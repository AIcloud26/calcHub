How to Calculate BMI: Complete Guide

January 15, 2024 • 8 min read

In this guide: Learn what BMI is, how to calculate it, what your score means, and its limitations as a health indicator.

What is BMI?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical value of your weight in relation to your height. It's a simple and inexpensive screening tool used to categorize people as underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese. While BMI doesn't directly measure body fat, it correlates reasonably well with more direct measures of body fat.

BMI Formula

The BMI formula differs slightly depending on the unit system you're using:

Metric Formula

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height² (m²)

Example: 70 kg ÷ (1.75 m)² = 70 ÷ 3.0625 = 22.86

Imperial Formula

BMI = (weight (lbs) ÷ height² (in²)) × 703

Example: (154 lbs ÷ 70² in²) × 703 = (154 ÷ 4900) × 703 = 22.09

BMI Categories

BMI categories are standardized for adult men and women:

Category BMI Range Health Risk
Underweight Below 18.5 Higher risk of nutritional deficiencies
Normal weight 18.5 – 24.9 Lowest risk of health problems
Overweight 25.0 – 29.9 Increased risk of health issues
Obese 30.0 and above Higher risk of serious health conditions

Limitations of BMI

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it has several limitations:

  • Doesn't distinguish muscle from fat: Athletes may have high BMI due to muscle mass, not excess fat
  • Doesn't account for fat distribution: Belly fat is more dangerous than fat stored in other areas
  • Age and gender considerations: BMI ranges differ for children and may not account for gender differences
  • Doesn't reflect overall health: Many factors contribute to health beyond weight
  • Ethnic differences: Different populations may have different health risks at the same BMI

Alternative Measurements

For a more complete picture of health, consider these additional measurements:

  • Waist circumference: Measures abdominal fat
  • Waist-to-hip ratio: Compares waist and hip measurements
  • Body fat percentage: Direct measurement of fat mass
  • Waist-to-height ratio: Simple indicator of health risk

Conclusion

BMI is a useful screening tool that provides a quick assessment of weight categories. However, it should be used as one part of a comprehensive health evaluation. Always consult with a healthcare professional for personalized health advice.
