Retirement Planning Basics: Start Early, Retire Happy

January 8, 2024 • 11 min read

In this guide: Learn why starting early matters, understand compound interest, and discover how to plan for a comfortable retirement.

Why Start Early?

The power of compound interest means that money invested early has more time to grow. Someone who starts investing $500/month at age 25 could have over $1.5 million by age 65, while someone who starts at 35 would have only about $750,000.

The 4% Rule

This popular retirement guideline suggests you can safely withdraw 4% of your retirement savings annually without running out of money for at least 30 years. This means if you need $50,000/year in retirement, you'd need $1.25 million saved.

Key Retirement Accounts

  • 401(k): Employer-sponsored, often with matching contributions
  • IRA: Individual retirement accounts with tax advantages
  • Roth accounts: Tax-free growth and withdrawals in retirement
