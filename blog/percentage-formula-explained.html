Percentage Formula Explained: Simple and Compound Percentages

January 5, 2024 • 8 min read

In this guide: Master percentage calculations with clear explanations and practical examples for everyday use.

What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The symbol "%" means "per hundred." So 25% means 25 out of every 100.

Basic Percentage Formulas

1. Finding X% of Y

Result = (X / 100) × Y

Example: What is 15% of 200? → (15/100) × 200 = 30

2. Finding What Percent X is of Y

Percentage = (X / Y) × 100

Example: What percent is 30 of 200? → (30/200) × 100 = 15%

3. Percentage Change

Change = ((New - Old) / Old) × 100

Example: $50 to $65? → ((65-50)/50) × 100 = 30% increase

Compound Percentages

Compound percentages are applied multiple times, like when calculating interest that compounds over time or price markups/markdowns.

Compound Increase

Final = Initial × (1 + rate)^n

Example: $1000 at 10% for 3 years → $1000 × 1.1³ = $1331

Practical Applications

  • Shopping: Calculating discounts and sale prices
  • Finance: Interest rates, investment returns, inflation
  • Health: Body composition, calorie tracking
  • Business: Profit margins, growth rates

Conclusion

Percentages are essential in everyday life. Once you understand the basic formulas, you can apply them to countless situations.
